How to Prepare for Your Corporate Headshot Session: A Complete Guide

A professional headshot is more than just a photo—it's an investment in your personal brand and career. Here's your comprehensive guide to ensuring you get the most out of your corporate headshot session.

Wardrobe Selection

Choose clothing that reflects your industry and personal brand while remaining timeless. Solid colors typically photograph best, with navy blue, charcoal gray, and deep burgundy being particularly effective choices. Avoid busy patterns or trendy styles that may date quickly. Bring multiple options to your session—we recommend:

  • 2-3 professional jackets or blazers
  • Several solid-colored shirts or blouses
  • Classic accessories that don't distract
  • Both formal and business casual options

Grooming and Preparation

Schedule any haircuts 1-2 weeks before your session to allow your style to settle naturally. For makeup, opt for a natural, professional look that enhances your features without appearing overdone. Men should shave or trim facial hair the morning of the shoot. Key preparation points include:

  • Get adequate rest the night before
  • Stay hydrated for several days leading up to the shoot
  • Bring touch-up supplies for last-minute adjustments
  • Consider professional makeup services for optimal results

Posing and Expression

The key to natural-looking headshots is feeling comfortable and confident. We'll guide you through various poses, but here are some fundamentals to keep in mind:

  • Practice your smile in the mirror to find your most natural expression
  • Maintain good posture without appearing stiff
  • Slightly angle your body rather than facing the camera directly
  • Keep your chin slightly forward to define your jawline

Day-of Session Tips

Arrive 10-15 minutes early to settle in and review your options with the photographer. Bring a small kit containing:

  • Hair brush or comb
  • Makeup for touch-ups
  • Lint roller
  • Any specific products you might need

Making the Most of Your Session

Communication with your photographer is crucial. Don't hesitate to:

  • Share your preferences and concerns
  • Ask to review images during the session
  • Request adjustments to lighting or angles
  • Discuss specific use cases for your photos
